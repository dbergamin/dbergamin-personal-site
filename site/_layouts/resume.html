Career Summary

I started out working part-time as a support engineer/junior dev for an internal J2EE-based product at ServiceRocket, while completing my Bachelor of IT at UTS. After graduating, I moved to the consulting team at ServiceRocket, selling, speccing and delivering process improvements, migrations and custom add-on development for the Atlassian ALM tools, and then tossing them over the 'wall' to customers ops teams.

Craving the satisfaction of longer-term ownership and continuous longer-term feature delivery, I moved to Transport for NSW, first building out a greenfield platform to deliver a revamped, cloud native update to their primary customer facing digital property transportnsw.info, and then operating and improving the platform and website in production for a year and a half. While at Transport, I co-operatively ran the Sydney Atlassian User Group (now ACE) events for a year to stay connected with my Atlassian product roots, doing event planning, hosting and delivering a few talks when community speakers were not available.

2018 brought about a move to London, where I started my consultancy, Digital Frontiers. The first engagagement with WorldFirst brought me into the ops sysadmin territory I had missed by jumping straight from development to the self-healing immutable infrastructure and serverless solutions we ran at Transport.

At WorldFirst, I continously delivered and supported software on bare-metal KVM hosts running in co-lo datacentres for the first 6 - 12 months. I then took a second engagement, which utilised my prior public cloud experience and knowledge of WorldFirst's production systems to provide technical leadership for a successful public cloud migration. A final engagment followed, where I led the updates and re-certification of IT BCP/DR plans for the new cloud platform.

I'm always on the lookout for new challenges. Please get in touch if you'd like to collaborate on a passion project, engage Digital Frontiers commercially, or just have a chat!
